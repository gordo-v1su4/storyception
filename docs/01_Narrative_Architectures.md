# SYSTEM DOC: NARRATIVE ARCHITECTURES & MAPPING
VERSION: 2026.1
PURPOSE: Defines the narrative structures used by the Storyboard Engine.

## 1. THE THREE-ACT STRUCTURE (Standard) - 9 Beats
*Use for: General Video, Corporate, Short Stories.*
- **Act I (Setup):**
  - Beat 1: Exposition (Establish the protagonist's ordinary world)
  - Beat 2: Inciting Incident (An event disrupts the ordinary world and kicks off the main story)
  - Beat 3: Plot Point 1 (The protagonist commits to facing the conflict, crossing a threshold into Act II)
- **Act II (Confrontation):**
  - Beat 4: Rising Action (The protagonist faces escalating challenges, and the stakes are raised)
  - Beat 5: Midpoint (A major turning point flips the story upside down, often making success feel impossible)
  - Beat 6: Plot Point 2 (The protagonist suffers a major setback, forcing them to question their ability to succeed)
- **Act III (Resolution):**
  - Beat 7: Pre-Climax (The protagonist regroups and prepares for the final confrontation)
  - Beat 8: Climax (The ultimate showdown where the central conflict is finally resolved)
  - Beat 9: DÃ©nouement (Loose ends are tied up, and the new status quo is revealed)

## 2. SAVE THE CAT (Cinematic) - 15 Beats
*Use for: Trailers, Screenplays, High-Pacing.*
- Beat 1: Opening Image (A snapshot that sets the tone and introduces the protagonist)
- Beat 2: Setup (The world is introduced, establishing relationships and stakes)
- Beat 3: Theme Stated (The story's deeper message or lesson is hinted at)
- Beat 4: Catalyst (The inciting incident that kicks the story into motion)
- Beat 5: Debate (The protagonist hesitates or questions the path ahead)
- Beat 6: Break into Two (The hero commits fully to the central goal, entering Act II)
- Beat 7: B Story (A subplot emerges, often focusing on an emotional thread like romance or friendship)
- Beat 8: Fun and Games (The "promise of the premise" is explored as the hero navigates their new world)
- Beat 9: Midpoint (A major twist or revelation changes the story's trajectory)
- Beat 10: Bad Guys Close In (Tension ramps up as obstacles and enemies surround the protagonist)
- Beat 11: All Is Lost (A crushing setback makes the protagonist confront their deepest fears)
- Beat 12: Dark Night of the Soul (The hero hits rock bottom and questions everything)
- Beat 13: Break into Three (A new insight sparks a path forward into Act III)
- Beat 14: Finale (The climax, where the hero uses everything they've learned to face the final challenge)
- Beat 15: Final Image (A closing snapshot that mirrors the opening image, showing how much the hero has transformed)

## 3. STORY CIRCLE (Character) - 8 Beats
*Use for: Character Arcs, Ads, Music Videos.*
- Beat 1: You (Zone of Comfort) - The character is grounded in their mundane and unchallenging everyday life
- Beat 2: Need (Want Something) - A core desire compels the protagonist to take action
- Beat 3: Go (Enter an Unfamiliar Situation) - The character crosses a threshold to pursue what they want
- Beat 4: Search (Adapt to It) - They must acquire new skills and learn how to survive in this new world
- Beat 5: Find (Get What They Wanted) - The character achieves their goal, but it comes at a significant cost
- Beat 6: Take (Pay a Heavy Price) - Victory is followed by new and unexpected losses or sacrifices
- Beat 7: Return (Return to Familiar Situation) - The character goes back to where they started
- Beat 8: Change (Having Changed) - The character has grown, and the lessons learned remain with them

## 4. HERO'S JOURNEY (Epic) - 12 Beats
*Use for: Gaming, Fantasy, Long-form.*
- **Part 1: Departure**
  - Beat 1: The Ordinary World (The hero's normal life is established before the adventure begins)
  - Beat 2: The Call to Adventure (An inciting incident disrupts the hero's comfort zone)
  - Beat 3: Refusal of the Call (The hero hesitates or resists the journey ahead)
  - Beat 4: Meeting the Mentor (A guide appears to offer wisdom, tools, or inspiration)
  - Beat 5: Crossing the First Threshold (The hero fully commits and steps into the unknown)
- **Part 2: Initiation**
  - Beat 6: Tests, Allies, Enemies (The hero faces challenges, gains allies, and identifies adversaries)
  - Beat 7: Approach to the Inmost Cave (The hero nears their goal, but danger and uncertainty increase)
  - Beat 8: The Ordeal (A critical confrontation forces the hero to face their deepest fears)
  - Beat 9: Reward (Seizing the Sword) (The hero earns a tangible or intangible boon after overcoming the ordeal)
- **Part 3: The Return**
  - Beat 10: The Road Back (The hero begins the journey home, but the story is not yet over)
  - Beat 11: Resurrection (The hero faces a final, defining climactic challenge)
  - Beat 12: Return with the Elixir (The hero returns to the ordinary world, transformed and bringing new knowledge or strength)

## 5. SEVEN-POINT STORY STRUCTURE (Milestone) - 7 Beats
*Use for: Discovery Writers, Flexible Outlining.*
- Beat 1: Hook (Grounds readers in the protagonist's world while introducing a spark of intrigue)
- Beat 2: Plot Point One (The inciting incident that disrupts normal life and pushes the protagonist into the main conflict)
- Beat 3: Pinch Point One (The first real clash with the antagonist, forcing the protagonist to confront the stakes)
- Beat 4: Midpoint (A major shift in perspective where the protagonist takes full responsibility and begins driving the story)
- Beat 5: Pinch Point Two (Conflict deepens and hope fades as the protagonist hits their lowest moment)
- Beat 6: Plot Point Two (A breakthrough where the hero gains new information, tools, or allies that change everything)
- Beat 7: Resolution (The climax and conclusion, where the protagonist's journey reaches its peak)

## 6. LESTER DENT'S MASTER FICTION PLOT (Pulp Fiction) - 19 Beats
*Use for: Thrillers, Pulp Fiction, Fast-Paced Action.*
- **Part 1: A Heap of Trouble**
  - Beat 1: Hit the Hero with Trouble (First line: Hit the hero with their trouble. Expand on the situation and hint at the core problem)
  - Beat 2: Jump into Action (The hero must grapple with the problem head-on from the start)
  - Beat 3: Introduce Allies and Counterparts (Bring in other key characters to engage with the problem)
  - Beat 4: Altercation 1 (Trigger a physical conflict to escalate events)
  - Beat 5: Achieve Something Minor (Allow the hero a small victory, like uncovering a clue or releasing a captive)
  - Beat 6: Plot Twist 1 (End the section with a surprise that reveals something is not what it seems)
- **Part 2: Double the Trouble**
  - Beat 7: Double the Trouble (Dramatically escalate the stakes and tension from Part 1)
  - Beat 8: Show the Struggle (Depict the hero unsuccessfully grappling with the heightened pressure)
  - Beat 9: Altercation 2 (Initiate a second physical conflict, keeping it fresh and distinct from the first)
  - Beat 10: Plot Twist 2 (End with another twist, proving yet another element is not as it seems)
- **Part 3: No Way Out**
  - Beat 11: Ratchet the Tension (Push the pressure to its absolute highest point)
  - Beat 12: Provide False Hope (Offer a temporary glimmer of hope or progress)
  - Beat 13: Altercation 3 (Ensure this glimmer of hope leads directly into a third physical conflict)
  - Beat 14: Devastating Twist (End with a crushing reversal that leaves the hero in an impossible, all-is-lost situation)
- **Part 4: Escape and Resolution**
  - Beat 15: Add a "Last Straw" (Push the hero to their absolute lowest point)
  - Beat 16: Escape and Defeat (The hero uses specialist skills to escape and overcome the villain)
  - Beat 17: Tie Up Loose Ends (Resolve all remaining mysteries)
  - Beat 18: Final Plot Twist (Deliver one last surprise at the very end)
  - Beat 19: Deliver the Punchline (Conclude with a clever, snappy final line)
